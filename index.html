Coffee-Beans

Arabica (Coffea arabica)


Flavor Profile

Sweet, soft, and fruity with hints of sugar; often has a floral aroma.


Characteristics

Higher acidity, more aromatic; grows best at high altitudes; accounts for about 60-70% of coffee production.

Robusta (Coffea canephora)


Flavor Profile

Strong, bitter, and earthy flavor with nutty undertones; less acidity.


Characteristics

Higher caffeine content than Arabica; more resilient and disease-resistant; commonly used in espresso blends.

Liberica (Coffea liberica)


Flavor Profile

Unique, floral, and fruity with a woody or smoky taste; less common.


Characteristics

Larger, irregularly shaped beans; grows well in tropical climates; often used in blends.

Excelsa (Coffea excelsa)


Flavor Profile

Complex, fruity, and tart with deep body; often described as dark and mysterious.


Characteristics

Smaller beans; used to add depth to coffee blends; primarily grown in Southeast Asia.
